## Limitations of Extrapolation

### Why These Predictions Are Unreliable

**1. Cross-Sectional Data Problem**
- We have models from 2026, not historical data from 2015-2026
- Cannot fit time series models (ARIMA, exponential smoothing)
- Cannot observe actual trends over time
- Extrapolating from current distribution is naive

**2. Linear Extrapolation Fallacy**
- Technology progress is rarely linear
- S-curves (logistic growth) are more realistic
- Plateaus and breakthroughs violate linear assumptions
- Moore's Law is slowing down (not exponential forever)

**3. Black Swan Events**
- GPT-4 level breakthroughs are unpredictable
- New architectures (transformers in 2017) change everything
- Regulatory shocks (EU AI Act, US executive orders)
- Economic disruptions (recessions, funding crunches)

**4. Competitive Dynamics**
- New entrants (DeepSeek, Mistral) can disrupt quickly
- incumbents (OpenAI, Google) may leapfrog
- Open-source models (Llama, Mistral) change economics
- Price wars could accelerate faster than predicted
